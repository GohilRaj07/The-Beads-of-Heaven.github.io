Our Craft

Made the way a mala has always been made

The Beads of Heaven manufactures kanthi and japa mala for devotees, temples, ashrams and retailers. A kanthi is worn at the neck as a mark of surrender and protection; a mala of 108 beads is held in the hand and turned once for every name repeated in prayer. Both have been made the same way for generations, and we haven't changed that.

Our workshop selects wood by hand, rounds and sizes every bead to match its neighbours, and strings each strand with a traditional knot between beads, so that no bead can slip, chip, or fall out of count.

How It's Made

From wood to worn

Five stages, every one of them by hand.

01

Sourcing

Tulsi stems, rudraksha seeds and sandalwood are selected for grain, density and age.

02

Shaping

Wood is cut and rounded into beads, then sorted so each strand is even in size.

03

Polishing

Beads are smoothed and given a natural finish, without paint or artificial colour.

04

Stringing

Each bead is knotted individually onto the cord, in the traditional counting order.

05

Final Check

Every strand is counted, tested for strength, and inspected before it leaves the workshop.
